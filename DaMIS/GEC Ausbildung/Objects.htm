Definition

ABATIS

A vehicular obstacle constructed by felling trees (leaving a 1-2 meter stump above the ground on both sides of a road, trail, gap, or defile) so that they fall, interlocking, toward the expected direction of enemy approach. The trees should remain attached to the stumps, be at a 45 degree angle to the roadway, and the obstacle itself should be at least 75 meters in depth to be most effective.

ABSOLUTE-FREQUENCY

A FREQUENCY that specifies a single frequency on a frequency scale.

ABSOLUTE-POINT

A POINT that has its coordinates specified with respect to a given frame of reference.

ACOUSTIC-CENTER-FREQUENCY

A frequency value which denotes the centre of the frequency range which is processed by an ACOUSTIC-FEATURE.

ACOUSTIC-FEATURE

A FEATURE which describes a phenomenon with respect to the use of acoustic energy.

ACTION-TASK

ACTION-TASK-STATUS

ACTION-TEMPORAL-ASSOCIATION

ADDRESS

ACPs are generally designated at each point where the flight route or air corridor makes a definite change in direction and any other point deemed necessary for timing or control of the operation.

AIR-DEFENCE-UNIT-TYPE

Designated to nullify or reduce effectiveness of hostile air assets.

AIR-DEFENCE-WEAPON

A weapon specifically designed for the engagement of aircraft.

AIR-LIAISON-OFFICER

A tactical air force or naval aviation officer attached to a ground or naval unit or formation as the advisor on tactical air operation matters.

AIR-LOADING-PLAN

The assignment of aggregated passengers, cargo and baggage for shipment with an aircraft.

AIR-MANOEUVRE-CAPABILITY

A CAPABILITY, required for planning, of those OBJECT-ITEMs or OBJECT-TYPEs that are deemed as having the nominal ability to move through the air by means of wings or wing-like parts.

AIR-MOVEMENT-OFFICER

An officer trained for duties in air movement/traffic sections.

AIR-ROUTE

AIR-SURVEILLANCE-OFFICER

An individual responsible for coordinating and maintaining an accurate, current picture of the air situation within an assigned airspace area.

AIR-TERMINAL

A facility on an airfield that functions as an air transportation hub and accomodates the loading and unloading of airlift aircraft and the intransit processing of traffic.

AIR-TRACK

A TRACK connecting the successive positions of an object or phenomenon as it moves through air.

AIR-TRAFFIC-CONTROL-FACILITY

Any of the component airspace control facilities primarily responsible for providing air traffic control services and, as required, limited tactical control services.

AIR-TRAFFIC-CONTROLLER

An air controller especially trained for an assigned to the duty of airspace management and traffic control of airborne objects.

AIR-WEAPONS-CONTROLLER

An individual especially trained for and assigned to the duty of employing and controlling air weapon systems against airborne and surface objects.

AIRCRAFT-ARRESTING-BARRIER

An installation that serves to stop an aircraft by absorbing its forward momentum in an emergency landing or an aborted takeoff.

AIRCRAFT-ARRESTING-WIRE

A wire installation which is spans the runway surface or flight deck landing area to absorb the forward momentum of a landing aircraft.

AIRCRAFT-COMMANDER

The aircrew member designated by competent authority as being in command of an aircraft and responsible for its safe operation and accomplishment of the assigned mission.

AIRFIELD

An area prepared for the accommodation (including any buildings installations,or equipment) landing and take off of aircraft.

AIRFIELD/AIRPORT/AIRSTRIP

An area prepared for the accommodation (including any buildings installations,or equipment) landing and take off of aircraft.

AIRHEAD

A designated area in a hostile or threatened territory which, when seized and held, ensures the continuous air landing of troops and material and provides maneuver space for operations. Normally it is the area seized in the assault phase of an airborne or air assault operation. Army--The airhead contains enough drop zones (DZs), landing zones (LZs), and extraction zones (EZs) to ensure mass, interior lines of communication and defense in depth.

AIRSPACE

A volume of space within which an aircraft may be subject to air traffic control.

AIRSPACE-COORDINATION-AREA

The airspace coordination area is represented by the general ACA symbol outlined by a rectangle.

AIRSPACE-LAYER

An airspace with a LAYER location

AIRSPACE-SECTOR

A subdivision of an airspace.

AIRWAY

A control area or portion thereof established in the form of a corridor marked with radio navigational aids.

ALLIED-MILITARY

ALTERNATE-DECONTAMINATION-FACILITY

AMMUNITION-CACHE

[No definition given in APP-6A]

The location for physical transfer of ammunition from one means of transport to another or to the final receiving unit.

ANTI-TANK-DITCH

AREA-OF-COVERAGE

The summation of geographical areas under surveillance or protected by supporting fire.

AREA-OF-INTEREST

A CONTROL-FEATURE-TYPE with an area location which denotes that area of concern to the commander, including the area of influence, and extending into enemy territory to the objectives of current or planned operations. This area also includes areas occupied by enemy forces who could jeopardise the accomplishment of the mission.

AREA-OF-OPERATIONS

That portion of an area necessary for military operations and for the administration of such operations. Army--A geographical area, usually defined by lateral, forward, and rear boundaries assigned to a commander, by a higher commander, in which he has responsibility and the authority to conduct military operations.

AREA-OF-PROBABILITY

[No definition inserted]

AREA-OF-RESPONSIBILITY

A CONTROL-FEATURE-TYPE with an area location which defines an area of land in which responsibility is specifically assigned to the commander of the area for the development and maintenance of installations, control of movement and the conduct of tactical operations involving troops under his control along with parallel authority to exercise these functions.

AREA-SMOKE-TARGET

Area in which fires with smoke bombs or rockets degrades temporarily the capability of enemy forces to see within.

ARMOURED-VEHICLE

A vehicle that has some form of ballistic protection (excluding tanks).

ARTILLERY-AREA

An area assigned to an M109A6 "Paladin" platoon and other artillery units for terrain management purposes in which the artillery maneuvered.

ARTILLERY-LOCATING-SITE

A facility containing equipment employed for locating artillery.

ASSAULT-POSITION

That position between the line of departure (LD) and the objective in an attack from which forces assault the objective. Ideally, it is the last covered and concealed position before reaching the objective.

ASSEMBLY-AREA

1. An area in which a command is assembled preparatory to further action. 2. In a supply installation, the gross area used for collecting and combining components into complete units, kits, or assemblies.

ASSOCIATION-REQUEST

To discover the relation between two ACTION-OBJECTIVEs (OBJECT-ITEMs) by any means.

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE is a subtype of TEMPLATE and represents associations between different TEMPLATEs. E.g. ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE may be used to describe an ADatP-3 message set as it is built from multiple ADatP-3 message fields (which may be described as ELEMENTARY-TEMPLATEs).

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENT

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENT is a certain element of ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE and represents the 'components' (TEMPLATEs) which contribute to a specific ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE.

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENT-ITERATION

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENT-ITERATION is a subtype of ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENTand represents an element of a certain iteration.

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENT-SEQUENCED-LIST

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENT-SEQUENCED-LIST is a subtype of ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENTand represents an element of a certain ordered list.

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENT-UNSEQUENCED-LIST

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENT-UNSEQUENCED-LIST is a subtype of ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENTand represents an element of a certain non-ordered list.

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ITERATION

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ITERATION is a substructure of ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE in which the "parent" ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE has exactly one "child" ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENT that is to be repeated.

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-SEQUENCED-LIST

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-SEQUENCED-LIST is a substructure of ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE in which the "parent" ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE has an -ordered set of "child" ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENTs.

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-UNSEQUENCED-LIST

ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-UNSEQUENCED-LIST is a substructure of ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE in which the "parent" ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE has a not-ordered set of "child" ASSOCIATIVE-TEMPLATE-ELEMENTs.

ATMOSPHERE

A METEOROLOGIC-FEATURE that specifies humidity, pressure, and temperature characteristics of Earth's atmosphere.

ATTACK-BY-FIRE-POSITION

Fires employed to destroy the enemy from a distance, normally used when the mission does not dictate or support occupation of the objective. This task is usually given to the supporting element during the offensive and as a counterattack option for the reserve during defensive operations.

ATTACK-HELICOPTER-COMMANDER

The designated commander of an attack helicopter unit.

ATTACK-POSITION

The last position occupied or passed through by the assault echelon before crossing the line of departure (LD).

AUTHORISED-COMMANDER

A duly appointed officer in charge of a unit, post, camp, or operation.

AXIS-OF-ADVANCE

A general area through which the bulk of a units combat power must move to draw the enemies attention away from the location of the commanders decisive operation. Forces conducting a feint make direct fire contact with the enemy.

AXIS-OF-ROTATION

A specified axis around which a specific MATERIEL is rotating.
