Attribut: combat-unit-type-arm-code

| Index | 


Definition
The specific value that represents or denotes the arm designation of a particular COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE.
Attributwerte
Air cavalry A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE especially equipped to combine movement by air with reconnaissance in depth.
Airborne/parachute A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE especially trained and equipped to effect, following transport by air, an assault debarkation either by parachuting or touchdown.
Anti-aircraft gun A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE whose principal role is the employment of gun systems against hostile air assets.
Anti-aircraft missile A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE whose principal role is the employment of missile systems against hostile air assets.
Anti-aircraft, not otherwise specified A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE whose principal role is the employment of weapon systems against hostile air assets.
Anti-tank A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE whose principal role is the employment of direct fire weapon systems against armoured vehicles other than from tanks.
Armour A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE equipped with tanks or other armoured direct fire weapon systems.
Armoured infantry A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE equipped with tracked armoured personnel carriers.
Armoured reconnaissance A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE equipped with armoured vehicles, whose principal role is the capture of tactical information.
Army aviation A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE equipped with helicopters capable of performing a variety of battlefield functions, including reconnaissance, air operations, and tactical lift.
Artillery, not otherwise specified A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE employing an unspecified type of indirect fire weapon.
Attack helicopter A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE equipped with armed helicopters whose principal role is the defeat of armour.
Cavalry A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE moving in lightly armoured vehicles and structured primarily to perform ground reconnaissance and security missions.
Field artillery A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE using tube artillery in support of manoeuvre units.
Infantry (light/non-mechanised) A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE comprising non-mechanised or lorry-(truck-)borne infantry.
Light armour A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE equipped with light-armoured vehicles, whose role is to close with and destroy the enemy.
Mechanised infantry A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE whose vehicles provide protection only against small arms and provide limited offensive capability.
Mortar A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE using indirect fire weapon capable only of high angles of fire.
Not known It is not possible to determine which value is most applicable.
Observer/controller A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE whose role is military observation and possibly local control of military operations.
Observer/controller, artillery A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE associated with an artillery unit specially equipped to carry out the control and adjustment of fire on the basis of observation.
Reconnaissance A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE whose role is the capture of tactical information but whose elements are on foot or mounted in soft-skin vehicles.
Rocket artillery A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE that uses missile-based indirect fire systems.
Special force A COMBAT-UNIT-TYPE whose role is to conduct unconventional warfare operations. This may include organising, training, supplying, directing and controlling indigenous forces.
