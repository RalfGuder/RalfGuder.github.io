Attribut: consumable-materiel-type-description-code

| Index | 


Definition
The specific value that represents or denotes the detailed class of a specific CONSUMABLE-MATERIEL-TYPE.
Attributwerte
Air-to-air missile An air-launched guided missile for use against air targets.
Air-to-surface missile A self-propelled airborne munition which is guided automatically, or by remote control, fired from an aircraft at an object on the ground or sea.
Anti-helicopter mine A mine designed to cause damage to helicopters.
Anti-personnel mine A mine designed to cause casualties to personnel.
Anti-submarine-torpedo Torpedo designed to be used against subsurface targets
Anti-surface-torpedo Torpedo designed to be used against surface targets
Anti-tank guided weapon A guided weapon designed to immobilize or destroy a tank.
Anti-tank mine A mine designed to immobilize or destroy a tank.
Anti-tank mine with anti-handling device [No definition given in APP-6A]
Aviation fuel A petroleum fraction used as fuel in airplane engines.
Bandages/dressings Medical material applied to cover and protect an injury.
Base-located-fuse Fuse located in the base of a projectile or bomb.
Blood The red liquid circulating in the arteries and veins of man and the higher animals, by which the tissues are constantly nourished and renewed.
Bomb A case filled with explosive, inflammable material, poison gas, or smoke, etc., dropped from aircraft, or thrown or deposited by hand.
Boresafe-fuse Type of fuse having an interrupter in the explosive train that prevents a projectile from exploding until after it has cleared the muzzle of a weapon.
Chaff Radar confusion reflectors, which consist of thin, narrow metallic strips of various lengths and frequency responses, used to reflect echoes for confusion purposes.
Cruise-missile Guided missile, the major portion of whose flight path to its target is conducted at approximately constand velocity; depends on the dynamic reaction of air for lift and upon propulsion forces to balance the drag.
Depth-charge An antisubmarine weapon that consists essentially of a drum filled with explosives which is dropped near a target and descends to a predetermined depth where it explodes. Synonymous for depth bomb.
Diesel fuel A petroleum fraction intended to be used as fuel in diesel engines.
Explosive A substance that is intended to explode or causes an explosion.
Flares A specific ammunition producing a bright light used for confusion purposes of infrared detection devices.
Fuse, not otherwise specified A device which initiates an explosive train.
Gun shell A munition which is propelled from a barrel and thereafter follows a ballistic trajectory.
Hand grenade A small bomb designed to be thrown by hand or shot from a modified rifle or a grenade launcher.
Impact-action-fuse A fuse that is set in action by the striking of a projectile or bomb against an object, e.g., percussion fuse, contact fuse. Synonymous with direct action fuse.
Infrared-seeker A seeker using the infrared spectrum for target detection and tracking purposes.
Kerosene A mixture of liquid hydrocarbons, a commercial product of the distillation of petroleum intended to be used in kerosene based engines.
Land-mine A mine usually placed on or just below the surface of the ground and designed to be exploded usually by the weight of vehicles or troops passing over it.
Lubricant A material, usually an oil, used to lubricate machinery.
Matting Steel or other material used to construct a supporting surface over ground.
Medicine Any substance or preparation used in the treatment of disease.
Mine, not otherwise specified [No definition given in APP-6A]
NATO-standardised-fuel NATO standardized fuels in accordance with STANAG 1135
NBC kit The personal equipment and or medical supplies issued to a person for protection against and nuclear, biological or chemical contamination or exposure.

Nitrogen, liquid Compressed nitrogen in liquid form.
Not known It is not possible to determine which value is most applicable.
Oil A substance having the following characters (or most of them): viz. those of being liquid at ordinary temperatures, of a viscid consistence and characteristic smooth and sticky (unctuous) feel, lighter than water and insoluble in it, soluble in alcohol and ether, inflammable, chemically neutral.
Oxygen, gaseous A colorless, tasteless, odorless gaseous element that constitutes 21 percent of the atmosphere and is found in water, in most rocks and minerals, and in numerous organic compounds, that is capable of combining with all elements except the inert gases, that is active in physiological processes, and that is involved especially in combustion.
Oxygen, liquid Compressed oxygen in liquid form.
Petrol A refined petroleum product intended to be used as fuel in gasoline engines.
Propulsion A device to drive forward or onward by or as if by means of a force that imparts motion.
Proximity fuse A fuse wherein primary initiation occurs by remotely sensing the presence, distance or direction of a target or its associated environment by means of a signal generated by the fuse or emitted by the target, or by detecting a disturbance of a natural field surrounding the target.
Radio-seeker A seeker using the radio (radar) spectrum for target acquisition purposes, e.g. used in Anti-Radiation Missiles
Rations, combat A package of fixed portions of food allotted for persons.
Rations, fresh Fresh food allotted for persons.
Rations, tin Food preserved in a container allotted for persons.
Revetting material Steel or other supporting material used in constructing a wall of earth.
Rocket A munition which is self-propelled in flight whose trajectory or course whilst in flight cannot be controlled.
Rolling-airframe-missile A missile designed to intercept other missiles especially used for self defense of ships and land objects.
Seeker A device of an ammunition enabling the detection and tracking of a target, generating the necessary signals for the guidance and propulsion to deliver the explosives into the target area.
Self-destroying-fuse A fuse designed to burst a projectile before the end of its flight.
Shuttered-fuse A fuse in which inadvertent initiation of the detonator will not initiate either the booster or the burst charge.
Small-arms ammunition Ammunition designed for hand held weapons.
Softload Ordnance remaining on board of a controlled aircraft excluding bombs and guns; often referred to as ordnance of Class C.
Surface-to-air missile A self-propelled airborne munition which is guided automatically, or by remote control, fired from the ground or vessel at an aircraft or other airborne target.
Surface-to-surface missile A self-propelled airborne munition which is guided automatically, or by remote control, fired from the ground or vessel at an object on the ground or sea.
Telemeter Electronic device for measuring any quantity such as the distance of an object from an observer, pressure, speed, or temperature, and transmitting the result especially by radio to a distant station.
Time-based-fuse A fuse which contains a graduated time element to regulate the time interval after which the fuse will function.
Uniform A distinctive dress of uniform cut, materials, and colour worn by all the members of a particular naval, military, or other force to which it is recognised as properly belonging and peculiar.
Video-seeker A seeker using the visible spectrum for the target acquisition process, often combined with a shade pattern recognition automat
Warhead A warhead of a certain ammunition containing the material determined to engage a target. This can be conventional explosive or special material (nuclear, biological, chemical).
Warhead, biological A warhead containing biological agents to produce casualties in man or animals and damage to plants or materiel
Warhead, chemical A warhead containing chemical agents to kill, injure, or incapacitate for a significant period of time, man or animals, and deny or hinder the use of areas, facilities or materiel
Warhead, conventional A warhead containing conventional explosives (neither nuclear, biological nor chemical)
Warhead, nuclear A warhead capable of producing a nuclear reaction and release of energy.
Water, fit for human consumption H2O that has been filtered and processed to remove poisons/toxins harmful to humans.
Water, fit for medical use H2O that has been filtered and processed to remove poisons/toxins harmful to humans and that has also been sterilised to enable it to be used for medical purposes.
Water, fit for uses other than medical or human consumption H2O that may have a level of poisons/toxins or bacteria that make it unfit for human consumption or medicalUSAGE, but that may be used for other purposes.
Water-mine A mine swimming in the sea on or just below the surface and designed to be exploded by contact or via magnetic detection of ships and boats passing by.
Wire A pliable metallic strand or rod made in many lengths and diameters.
