Domain
Name: harbour-type-category-code
Definition: The specific value that represents the class of HARBOUR-TYPE.
Definition Source: WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition
Allowable Values:
Value Definition Source Physical Value Identifier
Canal or lake A harbour located in the interior portion of a canal or lake that is connected with the sea by a navigable waterway. WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition CANAL 1000001
Coastal (Breakwater) A coastal harbour lying behind a man-made breakwater constructed to provide shelter, or supplement inadequate shelter already provided by natural sources. WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition COASTB 1000002
Coastal (Natural) A coastal harbour sheltered from the wind and sea by virtue of its location within a natural coastal indentation or in the protective lee of an island, cape, reef or other natural barrier. WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition COASTN 1000003
Coastal (Tide Gates) A coastal harbour, the waters of which are constrained by locks or other mechanical devices in order to provide sufficient water to float vessels at all stages of the tide. WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition COASTT 1000004
Inland water way A harbour located in the interior portion of a river, canal or lake that is not connected with the sea by a navigable waterway. Adapted WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition INLAND 1000005
Open roadstead A port, which has no artificial barrier to provide shelter from the wind, sea and swell. WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition OPENRD 1000006
River (Basins) A river harbour in which slips for vessels have been excavated in the banks, obliquely or at right angles to the axis of the stream. WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition RIVERB 1000007
River (Natural) A harbour located on a river, the waters of which are not retained by any artificial means. The facilities may consist of quays or wharves parallel to the banks of the stream, or piers or jetties, which extend into the stream. WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition RIVERN 1000008
River (Tide Gates) A river harbour, the waters of which are constrained by locks or other mechanical devices in order to provide sufficient water to float vessels at all stages of the tide. WPI Pub. 150; 2005; Eighteenth Edition RIVERT 1000009
