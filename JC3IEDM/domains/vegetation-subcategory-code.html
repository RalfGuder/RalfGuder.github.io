Domain
Name: vegetation-subcategory-code
Definition: The specific value that represents the detailed type of vegetation.
Definition Source: MIP-NDAG
Allowable Values:
Value Definition Source Physical Value Identifier
Bamboo/cane Woody, treelike grass. STANAG 7074 BAMBOO 1000001
Botanical garden A cultural area where plants and/or trees are displayed. STANAG 7074 BTNCLG 1000002
Cropland An area that has been tilled for the planting of crops. STANAG 7074 CRPLND 1000003
Desert A waterless, desolate area of land with little or no vegetation typically covered with sand. ATP-45 - Def COD DESERT 1000004
Forest A dense growth of trees, plants and underbrush covering a large area. Adapted STANAG 7074 FOREST 1000005
Grass/scrub/brush Area composed of uncultured plants which may have some woody tissue. STANAG 7074 GRASS 1000007
Grassland An area composed of uncultured plants which have little or no woody tissue. STANAG 7074 GRSLND 1000006
Hedgerow A continuous growth of shrubbery planted as a fence, a boundary, or a wind break. STANAG 7074 HDGERW 1000008
Hops An area covered by the systematic planting of hop vines. STANAG 7074 HOPS 1000009
Jungle, coastal/estuary An area of jungle on generally swampy ground where movement is slow and difficult. AFM(UK) Vol 2 Part 2 JUNGCE 1000010
Jungle, cultivation Areas which include areas such as rice paddies which will tend to be open, flat, dirty and swampy to areas such as rubber plantations which offer similar characteristics to primary jungle except for the fact that the trees will be in straight lines. AFM(UK) Vol 2 Part 2 JUNGC 1000011
Jungle, primary An area in lowland tropics, which has heavy rainfall. It has a thick 3 tier canopy up to 60m high and a reasonably clear floor that may permit some vehicular movement. Visibility may not exceed 50m. AFM(UK) Vol 2 Part 2 JUNGP 1000012
Jungle, secondary An area that has thick undergrowth created through rapid growth after an area of primary jungle has been cleared, movement will be slow and noisy, heat increased due to a lack of canopy and visibility will be reduced to around 20m. AFM(UK) Vol 2 Part 2 JUNGS 1000013
Marsh An area of wet, often spongy ground that is subject to frequent or tidal inundations, but not considered to be continually under water. It is characterized by the growth of non woody plants and by the lack of trees. STANAG 7074 MARSH 1000014
Nursery A place where shrubs, flowers, plants and trees are grown for transplanting, seed or grafting. STANAG 7074 NURSRY 1000015
Oasis A small, isolated, fertile or green area in a desert region usually having a spring or well. STANAG 7074 OASIS 1000016
Orchard/plantation An area covered by systematic plantings of trees which yield fruits, nuts or other products. STANAG 7074 ORCHRD 1000017
Savannah A grassy plain in tropical and subtropical regions, with few trees. COD SAVNNH 1000018
Scrub/brush/bush A characterisation of an area with low-growing woody plants. Adapted STANAG 7074 SCRUB 1000019
Swamp A low lying saturated area covered with water all or most of the year, where accumulating dead vegetation does not rapidly decay. It can exist on flat-lying areas created by certain geomorphic environments. The vegetation mainly consists of hydrophytic trees and/or scrubs whose roots are adapted to wet conditions, with an open to very dense canopy closure. STANAG 7074 SWAMP 1000020
Trees Woody-perennial plants, having a self-supporting main stem or trunk. STANAG 7074 TREES 1000021
Tundra A prairie-like region in the Arctic and Subarctic zones which sustains a growth of low vegetation. STANAG 7074 TUNDRA 1000022
Vineyard An area covered by the systematic planting of grape vines. STANAG 7074 VNEYRD 1000023
