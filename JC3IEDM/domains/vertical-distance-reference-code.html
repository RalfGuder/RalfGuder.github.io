Domain
Name: vertical-distance-reference-code
Definition: The specific value that represents the reference system for a specific VERTICAL-DISTANCE.
Definition Source: MIP-NDAG
Allowable Values:
Value Definition Source Physical Value Identifier
Chart datum The vertical reference established as the lowest observed or theoretical sea level possible for the area, such as Mean Lower Low Water (MLLW) or Lowest Astronomical Tide (LAT). MIP-NDAG CHADAT 1000001
Local datum The vertical reference for a level, a point or an object considered as a point measured from a specific datum. ICAO LOCDAT 1000002
Mean sea level The vertical reference established as the average level of the ocean surface measured over a full metonic tidal cycle of 18.6 years. MIP-NDAG MNSLVL 1000003
Pressure datum QFE The VERTICAL-DISTANCE reference in terms of atmospheric pressure indicated by a pressure altimeter that is calibrated to read 0 at airfield runway ground level. STANAG 5516-Annex E PDQFE 1000004
Pressure datum QNH The VERTICAL-DISTANCE reference in terms of atmospheric pressure indicated by a pressure altimeter that is calibrated to read 0 at Mean Sea Level (MSL). STANAG 5516-Annex E PDQNH 1000005
Pressure datum standard atmosphere The VERTICAL-DISTANCE reference in terms of atmospheric pressure indicated by a pressure altimeter that is calibrated to read 0 at standard atmosphere pressure of 1013.2 millibars. Adapted ICAO 4444 PDSTDT 1000006
Topographic surface The vertical reference is the actual visible surface. This includes both terrain and water surfaces. NIMA Geospatial Sciences Vertical Datum TOPOSR 1000008
WGS84 geoid The vertical reference established as the surface of the WGS84 geoid. STANAG 2211 WGS84G 1000009
WGS84 reference ellipsoid The vertical reference established as the surface of the WGS84 ellipsoid. STANAG 2211 WGS84R 1000010
Water bottom The datum for VERTICAL-DISTANCE provided by the submerged surface at a point or area. MIP-NDAG WATBOT 1000007
