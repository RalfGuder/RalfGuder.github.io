Domain
Name: atmosphere-pressure-system-category-code
Definition: The specific value that represents the class of a pressure system in a particular ATMOSPHERE.
Definition Source: ATCCIS
Allowable Values:
Value Definition Source Physical Value Identifier
Cold Front The discontinuity at the forward edge of an advancing cold air mass that is displacing warmer air in its path. APP-6A OCT 98 CLDFRT 1000001
High pressure centre An area of high atmospheric pressure that has a closed circulation that is anticyclonic, i.e., as viewed from above, the circulation is clockwise in the Northern Hemisphere, counterclockwise in the southern Hemisphere, undefined at the Equator. APP-6A OCT 98 HGHPRS 1000002
Low pressure centre 1. An area of low atmospheric pressure that has a closed circulation that is cyclonic, i.e., as viewed from above, the circulation is counterclockwise in the Northern Hemisphere, clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere, undefined at the Equator. Because cyclonic circulation and relatively low atmospheric pressure usually coexist, in common practice the terms cyclone and low are used interchangeably. Also, because cyclones often are accompanied by inclement (sometimes destructive) weather, they are frequently referred to simply as storms. 2. Frequently misused to denote a tornado. 3. In the Indian Ocean, a tropical cyclone of hurricane or typhoon force. APP-6A OCT 98 LOWPRS 1000003
Occluded front The line along which a cold front has overtaken a warm front at ground level. APP-6A OCT 98 OCCFRT 1000004
Ridge line An elongated region of high atmospheric pressure. APP-6A OCT 98 RDGLIN 1000005
Squall line A line of high winds and thunderstorms in convectively unstable air, an instability line (of non-frontal nature); it may be generated by a cold front. APP-6A OCT 98 SQLLIN 1000006
Stationary front A situation in which the surface position of a front does not move; the flow on either side of such a boundary is nearly parallel to the position of the front. APP-6A OCT 98 STAFRT 1000007
Trough line An elongated region of low atmospheric pressure. APP-6A OCT 98 TRGLIN 1000008
Upper cold front Occurs when discontinuity at the forward edge of an advancing cold air mass is displacing warmer air in its path and the two air masses intersect above ground level. APP-6A OCT 98 UPCFRT 1000009
Upper warm front Occurs when discontinuity at the forward edge of an advancing warm air mass is displacing cooler air in its path and the two air masses intersect above ground level. APP-6A OCT 98 UPWFRT 1000010
Warm front The discontinuity at the forward edge of an advancing warm air mass that is displacing cooler air in its path. APP-6A OCT 98 WRMFRT 1000011
