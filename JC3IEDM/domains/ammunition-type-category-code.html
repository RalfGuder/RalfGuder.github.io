Definition: The specific value that represents the class of AMMUNITION-TYPE.
Definition Source: ATCCIS
Allowable Values:
Value Definition Source Physical Value Identifier
Air launched missile A self-propelled airborne munition that is guided automatically, or by remote control, fired from an aircraft. MIP-NDAG ALMIS 1000048
Air-to-air missile An air-launched guided missile for use against air targets. JCS Pub 1-02 AAMIS 1000001
Air-to-surface missile A self-propelled airborne munition which is guided automatically, or by remote control, fired from an aircraft at an object on the ground or sea. UK MOD ASMIS 1000002
Anti-tank guided weapon A guided weapon designed to immobilise or destroy a tank. JCS Pub 1-02 ATGDWP 1000003
Bomb A case filled with explosive, inflammable material, poison gas, or smoke, etc., dropped from aircraft, or thrown or deposited by hand. APP-6A OCT 98 BOMB 1000004
Bomblet One of a number of small bombs usually contained in a cluster bomb and released in mid-air. APP-9 - Def ATCCIS BOMBLT 1000005
Cruise missile A weapon in the form of a pilotless jet aircraft carrying a warhead and capable of flying at low altitudes. ADatP-3 BL12, BL13.1 FFIRN/FUD 1407/27 adapted - Def NDAG CRUMSL 1000037
Depth charge Any charge designed for explosion under water, especially such a charge dropped or catapulted from delivery equipment and used against submarines. OED DPTHCH 1000006
Explosive An explosive substance. OED EXPLOS 1000007
Gun shell A munition that is propelled from a barrel and thereafter follows a ballistic trajectory. UK MOD GNSHEL 1000008
Hand grenade An explosive missile, smaller than a bombshell, thrown by hand. AIntP-3 - Def adapted OED HNDGRN 1000009
Maritime mine disposal charge An explosive charge that is used to destroy a mine. ADatP-3 BL12.2, BL13.1 FFIRN/FUD 1044/189 adapted - Def MIP-NDAG/MWTU MRTMDC 1000045
Mine, anti-helicopter A mine designed to cause damage to helicopters. ATCCIS MINAHL 1000010

Mine, anti-tank A mine designed to immobilise or destroy a tank. APP-6A OCT 98 MINAT 1000012
Mine, anti-tank with anti-handling device A mine designed to immobilize or destroy a tank. The mine includes an internal or external device arranged to actuate it in case of attempts to deactivate the mine. APP-6A OCT 98 - Def adapted AAP 19 and AAP 6 MINATA 1000013
Mine, deep moored Any mine case in more than a certain depth of water is deep. ATP-24(B) MINDPM 1000038

Mine, moored A contact or influence-operated mine of positive buoyancy held below the surface by a mooring attached to a sinker or anchor on the bottom. ATP-24(B) - Def AAP-6(V) MINMOR 1000039
Mine, not known It is not possible to determine the type of mine. Adapted APP-9 MINNKN 1000014
Mine, not otherwise specified A mine whose appropriate value is not in the set of specified values. APP-6A OCT 98 - Def MIP-NDAG MINNOS 1000015
Mine, off-route A mine or mines designed to attack a target obliquely to the targets direction of travel (i.e. in the targets flank or side). Adapted APP-9 MINOFR 1000016

Mine, wide area A mine designed to disable armoured vehicles and will allow large areas to be sown with smart mines that should be difficult to neutralize. APP-9 adapted MINWAR 1000017
Missile, anti-radiation A missile that homes passively on a radiation source. ADatP-3 BL11, BL13.1 FFIRN/FUD 1650/6 adapted - Def AAP-6 APR 2008 MISATR 1000043
Missile, guided An unmanned self-propelled vehicle whose trajectory or course, while in flight, is controlled. ADatP-3 BL11, BL13.1 FFIRN/FUD 1650/6 - Def AAP-6 APR 2008 MISGUI 1000044
Mortar bomb A round fired from a mortar weapon. ATCCIS MRTRBM 1000018
Moving mine, sea The collective description of mines, such as drifting, oscillating, creeping, mobile, rising, homing and bouquet mines. ADatP-3 BL12 1140/1, BL13.1 FFIRN/FUD 1141/1 adapted - Def AAP-6 APR 2008 adapted MVSEAM 1000047
Projectile, not otherwise specified An object capable of being propelled by a force normally from a gun, and continuing in motion by virtue of its kinetic energy. AAP-6 PRJNOS 1000019
Proximity fuse A fuse wherein primary initiation occurs by remotely sensing the presence, distance or direction of a target or its associated environment by means of a signal generated by the fuse or emitted by the target, or by detecting a disturbance of a natural field surrounding the target. JCS Pub 1-02 PRXFUS 1000020
Pyrotechnic device A mixture of chemicals which when ignited is capable of reacting exothermically to produce light, heat, smoke, sound or gas, and may also be used to introduce a delay into an explosive train because of its known burning time. AAP-6 PYROTC 1000021
Rocket A munition that is self-propelled in flight whose trajectory or course whilst in flight cannot be controlled. UK MOD RCKET 1000022
Rocket propelled grenade An unguided rocket equipped with an explosive warhead. OPSITREP IA A011 Draft FFIRN/FUD 1058/1 - Def MIP-DMSWG RPG 1000049
Rocket/missile, anti-personnel A powered projectile designed for use against people. APP-6A OCT 98 - Def adapted OED RKTATP 1000023
Rocket/missile, anti-tank A powered projectile designed for use against tanks. APP-6A OCT 98 - Def adapted OED RKTATT 1000024
Rocket/missile, heavy A powered projectile designed for use by heavy launchers. APP-6A OCT 98 - Def adapted OED RKTHEV 1000025
Rocket/missile, light A powered projectile designed for use by light launchers. APP-6A OCT 98 - Def adapted OED RKTLGT 1000026
Rocket/missile, medium A powered projectile designed for use by medium launchers. APP-6A OCT 98 - Def adapted OED RKTMED 1000027
Sea mine An explosive device laid in the water with the intention of damaging or sinking ships or of deterring shipping from entering an area. Adapted JCS Pub 1-02 SEAMIN 1000028
Seabed mine A mine with negative buoyancy, which remains on the seabed. (bottom mine) AAP-6 SEABDM 1000029
Shaped charge A charge shaped so as to concentrate its explosive force in a particular direction. OPSITREP IA A011 Draft FFIRN/FUD 1058/1 - Def AAP-6 APR 2008 SHPCHG 1000050
Small-arms ammunition Ammunition designed for hand held weapons. Adapted JCS Pub 1-02 SMAMMO 1000030
Submunition Any munition that, to perform its task, separates from a parent munition. AAP-6 SUBMUN 1000033
Surface-to-air missile A self-propelled airborne munition which is guided automatically, or by remote control, fired from the ground or vessel at an aircraft or other airborne target. UK MOD SRAMIS 1000031
Surface-to-surface missile A self-propelled airborne munition which is guided automatically, or by remote control, fired from the ground or vessel at an object on the ground or sea. UK MOD SRSMIS 1000032
Torpedo A weapon for destroying ships by rupturing their hulls below the waterline. APP-9 - Def ATCCIS TRPEDO 1000034
Not known It is not possible to determine which value is most applicable. ATCCIS NKN 1000035
