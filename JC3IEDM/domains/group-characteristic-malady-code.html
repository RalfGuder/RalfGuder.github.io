Name: group-characteristic-malady-code
Definition: The specific value that identifies the type of ill health, ailment or disease in a specific GROUP-CHARACTERISTIC.
Definition Source: MIP-NDAG
Allowable Values:
Value Definition Source Physical Value Identifier
AIDS/HIV Acquired immune deficiency syndrome, a condition caused by a virus transmitted in the body fluids, marked by severe loss of resistance to infection and so ultimately fatal. WHO AIDS 1000002
African trypanosomiasis Any of several diseases caused by a trypanosome and usually transmitted by biting insects, including sleeping sickness and Chagas' disease. WHO AFRTRP 1000001
Amebiasis/Amoebic dysentry An acute inflammatory amebiasis of the colon, marked by severe pain and diarrhoea and caused by the amoeba Entamoeba Histolytica. WHO AMBDYS 1000003
Anthrax A bacterial disease caused by the spore-forming Bacillus anthracis, a Gram positive, rod-shaped bacterium. There are 3 types of anthrax in humans: cutaneous anthrax, acquired when a spore enters the skin through a cut or an abrasion; gastrointestinal tract anthrax, contracted from eating contaminated food, primarily meat from an animal that died of the disease; and pulmonary (inhalation) anthrax from breathing in airborne anthrax spores. WHO ANTHRX 1000004
Antimicrobial resistance Antimicrobial resistance, a condition encouraged by agricultural and animal uses of drugs and is due to under-use of drugs in poorer countries and over-use in wealthy countries. WHO ANTMRS 1000005
Arbovirus infection (NEC) Viruses that are maintained in nature principally, or to an important extent, through biological transmission between susceptible vertebrate hosts by haematophagous arthropods; they multiply and produce viraemia in the vertebrates, multiply in the tissues of arthropods and are passed on to the new vertebrates by the bites of arthropod after a period of extrinsic incubation. Examples are Dengue and Yellow Fever. WHO ARBVRS 1000006
Botulism Botulism is an intoxication caused by extremely potent toxins produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum preformed in foods. WHO BOTULM 1000007
Brucellosis A disease caused by bacteria of the genus Brucella usually transmitted from animals to man through ingestion, contact, inhalation or inoculation. WHO BRUCLS 1000008
Buruli ulcer A Mycobacterium ulcerans, whose source is unknown, that causes painless swelling in the skin and causes severely deforming ulcers. WHO BURULI 1000009
Campylobacter Campylobacters are bacteria that are a major cause of diarrhoeal illness in humans and are generally regarded as the most common bacterial cause of gastroenteritis worldwide. Campylobacteriosis is the disease caused by the presence of campylobacters. WHO CAMPLB 1000010
Chagas disease A disease caused by trypanosomes transmitted by bloodsucking bugs, endemic in S. America and causing damage to the heart and central nervous system. WHO CHAGAS 1000011
Chancroid A genital ulcer disease caused by the Gram-negative bacillus H. ducreyi and results in superficial ulcerations, often with suppurant regional lymphadenopathy. WHO CHANCR 1000012
Chicken pox An acute contagious viral disease, usually of young children, characterised by skin eruption and a slight fever. Adapted WHO CHCKPX 1000013
Chlamydia A virus-like bacterium of the genus Chlamydia, which comprises Gram-negative coccoids that reproduce as intracellular parasites in vertebrates and occasionally anthropods and is classified as a major cause of sexually transmited diseases (STDs). Adapted WHO CHLMDA 1000014
Cholera/epidemic dysentery An infectious and often fatal disease of the small intestine caused by the mainly water-borne bacterium Vibrio cholerae, resulting in severe vomiting and diarrhoea. WHO CHOLRA 1000015
Coccidioidomycosis A fungus disease that usually affects the lungs of humans and other animals, caused by the fungus Coccidioides immitis. Adapted WHO COCCID 1000016
Croup Croup is a viral infection that causes a brassy, barking cough in young children. Adapted WHO CROUP 1000017
Cryptosporidoiosis A diarrhoeal disease caused by a microscopic parasite, Cryptosporidium parvum. It is one of the most common causes of waterborne disease (drinking and recreational) in humans. Adapted CDC CRYPTS 1000018
Cyclospora A parasite composed of one cell, too small to be seen without a microscope. Cyclospora is spread by people ingesting something, for example, water or food that was contaminated with infected stool. Adapted CDC CYCLSP 1000019
Dengue An infectious viral disease of the tropics, transmitted by mosquitoes and causing a fever and acute pains in the joints. WHO DENGUE 1000020
Diptheria Diphtheria is a bacterial infection caused by Corynebacterium diphtheriae, and can occur as a toxic or nontoxic strain. It spreads through close contact with a person infected with diphtheria. Adapted CDC DIPTHR 1000021
Donovanosis A chronic, slowly progressive, mildly contagious disease of venereal origin, characterized by granulomatous ulceration of the genitalia and neighboring sites, with little or no tendency to spontaneous healing. WHO DONOVA 1000022
Dracunculiasis A parasitic disease caused by the parasitic worm Drarunculus medinensis or "guinea worm". WHO DRACUN 1000023
E. Coli 0157:H7/verotoxigenic E Coli A strain of the bacterium Escherichia coli that produces a powerful toxin and can cause severe illness. The infection often causes severe bloody diarrhoea and abdominal cramps; sometimes the infection causes non-bloody diarrhoea or no symptoms. Usually little or no fever is present, and the illness resolves in 5 to 10 days. Adapted CDC ECOLI 1000024
Ehrlichiosis A tick borne disease caused by several bacterial species in the genus Ehrlichia, characterized by fever and swollen lymph nodes. Adapted CDC EHRLCH 1000025
Encephalitis Viruses that are maintained in nature through biological transmission between susceptible vertebrate hosts by blood feeding arthropods (mosquitoes, psychodids, ceratopogonids, and ticks) usually resulting in a brain inflammation. Adapted CDC ENCPHL 1000026
Erysipelas A streptococcal infection of the skin and subcutaneous tissue caused by a streptococcus and marked by spreading inflammation. Adapted CDC ERYSPL 1000027
Filariasis A tropical disease caused by the presence of filarial worms esp. in the lymph vessels. WHO FILARS 1000028
Gastroenteritis (under 2s only) A rotavirus infection of the stomach and small and large intestines caused by a variety of viruses that results in vomiting or diarrhoea. Adapted CDC GASTRE 1000029
Giardiasis A diarrhoeal disease caused by a microscopic parasite, Giardia intestinalis that lives in the intestine of people and animals. It is one of the most common causes of waterborne disease (drinking and recreational) in humans. Adapted CDC GIARDS 1000030
Gonorrhea Gonorrhea, a common sexually transmitted disease (STD), is caused by Neisseria gonorrhoeae, a bacterium that can grow and multiply easily in mucous membranes of the body. Adapted CDC GONORH 1000031
H. influenzae, invasive A bacterial infection and one of the leading causes of bacterial meningitis in children under 5 years of age. Adapted CDC HINFLZ 1000032
HIV infection Human Immunodeficiency Virus, either of 2 retroviruses (HIV-1, HIV-2) that causes AIDS. Adapted CDC HIV 1000038
Hantavirus infection Hantaviruses are carried by rodents, especially the deer mouse. You can become infected by exposure to their droppings, and the first signs of sickness (especially fever and muscle aches) appear 1 to 5 weeks later, followed by shortness of breath and coughing. Once this phase begins, the disease progresses rapidly, necessitating hospitalisation and often ventilation within 24 hours. Adapted CDC HANTAV 1000033
Hemorrhagic fever A severe multisystem syndrome (multisystem in that multiple organ systems in the body are affected).  Characteristically, the overall vascular system is damaged, and the body's ability to regulate itself is impaired.  These symptoms are often accompanied by hemorrhage (bleeding); however, the bleeding is itself rarely life-threatening. Adapted CDC HEMRHG 1000034
Hepatitis A A liver disease caused by the hepatitis A virus (HAV). Adapted CDC HEPATA 1000035
Hepatitis B A serious disease caused by a virus that attacks the liver. The virus, which is called hepatitis B virus (HBV), can cause lifelong infection, cirrhosis (scarring) of the liver, liver cancer, liver failure, and death. Adapted CDC HEPATB 1000036
Hepatitis C A liver disease caused by the Hepatitis C virus (HCV), which is found in the blood of persons who have the disease. HCV is spread by contact with the blood of an infected person. Adapted CDC HEPATC 1000037
Human monkeypox A rare smallpox like disease acquired from monkeys or squirrels but does occasionally spread from man to man in unvaccinated communities. WHO HMNMPX 1000039
Hydatid infection A disease that results from being infected with the larval stage of Echinococcus multilocularis, a microscopic tapeworm (1-4 millimeters) found in foxes, coyotes, dogs, and cats. Adapted CDC HYDATD 1000040
Infective enteritis An intestinal inflammation. Adapted OED INFENT 1000041
Influenza A highly contagious virus infection causing fever, severe aching, and catarrh, often occurring in epidemics. WHO INFLUN 1000042
Legionellosis An infection caused by the bacterium Legionella pneumophila usually transmitted by mists that come from a water source (e.g., air conditioning cooling towers, whirlpool spas, showers) contaminated with Legionella bacteria. Adapted CDC LEGION 1000043
Leishmaniasis Any of several diseases caused by parasitic protozoans of the genus Leishmania transmitted by the bite of sandflies. WHO LSHMNA 1000044
Leishmaniasis, cutaneous A disease, resulting in skin sores, caused by the parasitic protozoans of the genus Leishmania transmitted by sand flies. Adapted CDC LSHMNC 1000045
Leishmaniasis, mucocutaneous A disease, resulting in sores around the nose or mouth, caused by the parasitic protozoans of the genus Leishmania transmitted by sand flies. Adapted CDC LSHMNM 1000046
Leishmaniasis, visceral A disease, resulting in damage to some of the internal organs of the body (for example, spleen, liver, bone marrow), caused by the parasitic protozoans of the genus Leishmania transmitted by sand flies. Adapted CDC LSHMNV 1000047
Leprosy A contagious bacterial disease that affects the skin, mucous membranes, and nerves, causing disfigurement. WHO LEPRSY 1000048
Leptospirosis Leptospirosis is a bacterial disease that affects humans and animals. It is caused by bacteria of the genus Leptospira found in water contaminated with the urine of infected animals. Adapted CDC LEPTOS 1000049
Listeriosis A serious infection caused by eating food contaminated with the bacterium Listeria monocytogenes. Adapted CDC LISTRS 1000050
Lyme disease An infectious disease caused by the bacterium, Borrelia burgdorferi, transmitted by the bite of infected deer ticks. Adapted CDC LYME 1000051
Lymphogranuloma venereum A disease, caused by infection with L1, L2, or L3 serovars of Chlamydia trachomatis, usually sexually transmitted, characterized by genital lesions, suppurative regional lymphadenopathy, or hemorrhagic proctitis. Adapted CDC LYMPHG 1000052
Malaria An intermittent and remittent fever caused by a protozoan parasite of the genus Plasmodium, introduced by the bite of a mosquito. WHO MALARA 1000053
Measles An acute infectious viral disease marked by red spots on the skin. WHO MEASLS 1000054
Meningitis An infection of the fluid of a person's spinal cord and the fluid that surrounds the brain. Adapted CDC MENING 1000055
Meningitis, other bacterial An infection of the fluid of a person's spinal cord and the fluid that surrounds the brain usually caused by a bacterial infection. Adapted CDC MENNGO 1000057
Meningitis, pneumonococcal An infection of the fluid of a person's spinal cord and the fluid that surrounds the brain usually caused by a Streptococcus pneumoniae bacteria. Adapted WHO MENNGP 1000058
Meningitis/encephalis viral An infection of the fluid of a person's spinal cord and the fluid that surrounds the brain usually caused by a viral infection. Adapted CDC MENNGE 1000056
Meningococcal disease An infection of the fluid of a person's spinal cord and the fluid that surrounds the brain usually caused by a Neisseria meningitidis bacteria. Adapted CDC MNNGOC 1000059
Mumps An acute viral illness acquired by respiratory droplets. Adapted CDC MUMPS 1000060
Ornithosis An illness characterized by fever, chills, headache, photophobia, cough, and myalgia. Adapted CDC ORNTHS 1000061
Paratyphoid fever An acute intestinal disease that resembles typhoid fever and is caused by any of three bacteria of the genus Salmonella. Adapted CDC PRTPHY 1000062
Pertussis An acute infectious disease (Whooping Cough) caused by the bacterium Bordetella pertussis, a small aerobic gram-negative rod. Adapted CDC PRTUSS 1000063
Plague A contagious bacterial disease characterized by fever and delirium, with the formation of buboes (bubonic plague) and sometimes infection of the lungs (pneumonic plague). WHO PLAGUE 1000064
Pneumonia, pneumococcal A disease that results from the infection of the air passages and lungs by certain germs. Adapted WHO PNEUMO 1000065
Poliomyelitis Acute onset of a flaccid paralysis of one or more limbs with decreased or absent tendon reflexes in the affected limbs, without other apparent cause, and without sensory or cognitive loss. Adapted CDC POLIO 1000066
Puerperal fever An infection of the genital tract and of the blood stream after birth. Adapted OED PUERPL 1000067
Q fever A zoonotic disease caused by Coxiella burnetii, a species of bacteria that is distributed globally. Adapted CDC QFEVER 1000068
Rabies, human A contagious and fatal viral disease of dogs and other mammals, transmissible through the saliva to humans etc. and causing madness and convulsions; hydrophobia. WHO RABIES 1000069
Relapsing fever An infection caused by the spirochetes Borrelia hermsii and B. turicatae and is transmitted to humans principally by the bites of the infected ticks. Adapted CDC RLPSNG 1000070
Rheumatic fever, acute An inflammatory illness that occurs as a delayed sequela of group A streptococcal infection. Adapted CDC RHEUMA 1000071
Rift valley fever An acute, fever-causing viral disease that affects domestic animals (such as cattle, buffalo, sheep, goats, and camels) and humans. RVF is most commonly associated with mosquito-borne epidemics during years of unusually heavy rainfall. Adapted CDC RIFTVF 1000072
Rocky mountain spotted fever A rickettsial illness caused by Rickettsia rickettsii, a species of bacteria that is spread to humans by ixodid (hard) ticks. Adapted CDC RCKYMT 1000073
Ross river virus infection A mosquito borne virus found only in Australia. Adapted CDC ROSSRV 1000074
Rubella A virus, also referred to as German Measles, classified as a togavirus, genus Rubivirus. Adapted CDC RUBELA 1000075
Rubella, congenital A Rubella infection characterized by congenital cataracts in infants born following maternal rubella infection in early pregnancy. Adapted CDC RUBELC 1000076
Salmonellosis An infection with a bacterium called Salmonella usually acquired from contaminated food, water, or contact with infected animals. Adapted CDC SALMNL 1000077
Scarlet fever A disease caused by a bacteria called group A streptococcus, usually transmitted by contact with the sick person because this germ is carried in the mouth and nasal fluids. Adapted CDC SCRLET 1000079
Schistosomiasis/intestinal parasites A parasitic tropical flatworm of the genus Schistosoma, carried by freshwater snails and infesting the blood vessels of birds and mammals, causing bilharzia in humans. WHO SCHSTO 1000080
Septicemia A systematic disease caused by pathogenic organisms or other toxins in the bloodstream. Adapted CDC SEPTCM 1000078
Severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS) A respiratory illness spread by direct close person-to-person contact with infectious material (for example, respiratory secretions) from a person who has SARS. Adapted WHO SARS 1000081
Sexually transmitted infections Any of various diseases contracted chiefly by sexual intercourse with a person already infected; a sexually transmitted disease. WHO - Def adapted COD SXLTRN 1000082
Shigellosis An infectious disease caused by a group of bacteria called Shigella, usually passing from person to person from stools or soiled fingers of one person to the mouth of another person. Adapted CDC SHGLLS 1000083
Smallpox An infectious disease caused by the variola virus usually transmitted from person to person or through direct contact with infected bodily fluids or contaminated objects such as bedding or clothing. Adapted CDC SMLLPX 1000084
Streptococcus, group A, invasive A bacterium often found in the throat and on the skin and spread through direct contact with mucus from the nose or throat of persons who are infected or through contact with infected wounds or sores on the skin. Adapted CDC STREPT 1000085
Syphilis (All) A complex sexually transmitted disease (STD) caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum usually passed from person to person through direct contact with a syphilis sore. Sores occur mainly on the external genitals, vagina, anus, or in the rectum. Adapted CDC SYPHLA 1000086
Syphilis, congenital A condition caused by infection in utero with Treponema pallidum. The infection of an infant whose mother had untreated or inadequately treated syphilis at delivery. Adapted CDC SYPHLC 1000087
Syphilis, early symptomatic A subcategory of latent syphilis. When initial infection has occurred within the previous 12 months, latent syphilis is classified as early latent. Adapted CDC SYPHLE 1000088
Syphilis, latent A stage of infection caused by T. pallidum in which organisms persist in the body of the infected person without causing symptoms or signs. Adapted CDC SYPHLL 1000089
Syphilis, primary/secondary A stage of infection with Treponema pallidum characterized by one or more chancres (ulcers); chancres might differ considerably in clinical appearance and by localized or diffuse mucocutaneous lesions, often with generalized lymphadenopathy. Adapted CDC SYPHLP 1000090
Tetanus An acute, often fatal, disease caused by exotoxin produced by Clostridium tetani. Spores are widely distributed in soil and in the intestines and feces of some animals. Usually enters the body through a wound. Adapted CDC TETANS 1000091
Toxic shock syndrome A toxin caused syndrome manifested by sudden onset of fever, chills, vomiting, diarrhoea, muscle pains and rash associated with use of tampons and intravaginal contraceptive devices in women and occurs as a complication of skin abscesses or surgery. Adapted CDC TOXSHK 1000092
Toxoplasmosis An infection caused by a single-celled parasite called Toxoplasma gondii, usually resulting from accidental ingestion of contaminated cat feces, or ingestion of raw or partly cooked meat, especially pork, lamb, or venison, or by touching your hands to your mouth after handling undercooked meat, or through contamination of knives, utensils, cutting boards and other foods that have had contact with raw meat. Adapted CDC TOXPLS 1000093
Transmissible spongiform encephalopathies A disease of the brain. WHO TRNSSP 1000094
Trichinosis Caused by eating raw or undercooked pork and wild game products infected with the larvae of a species of worm called Trichinella. Adapted CDC TRCHNS 1000095
Trypanosomiasis A parasite 'Trypanosoma brucei rhodesiense' transmitted disease (Sleeping Sickness) caused by the bite of the Tsetse fly. Adapted CDC TRYPNS 1000096
Tuberculosis, pulmonary A disease caused by bacteria called Mycobacterium tuberculosis. The bacteria can attack any part of your body, but they usually attack the lungs. It is spread through the air from one person to another. Adapted CDC TUBERC 1000097
Tularemia An infectious disease caused by a hardy bacterium, Francisella tularensis, found in animals (especially rodents, rabbits, and hares). It is contracted through the bite of an infected insect or other arthropod (usually a tick or deerfly), handling infected animal carcasses, eating or drinking contaminated food or water, or breathing in F. tularensis. Adapted CDC TULARM 1000098
Typhoid fever A life-threatening illness caused by the bacterium Salmonella Typhi, that lives only in humans. It can be contracted if you eat food or drink beverages that have been handled by a person who is shedding S. Typhi or if sewage contaminated with S. Typhi bacteria gets into the water you use for drinking or washing food. Adapted CDC TYPHOD 1000099
Typhus fever Any of several forms of an infectious disease caused by micro-organisms of the genus Rickettsia, especially when flea-borne as in endemic typhus, louse-borne as in epidemic typhus, or mite-borne as in scrub typhus. Adapted CDC TYPHUS 1000100
Urethritis, non-gonococcal Urethral inflammation that is not the result of infection with Neisseria gonorrhoeae. Adapted CDC URTHRT 1000101
Varicella, active duty only A virus of the herpes family that can result in bacterial infection of the skin, swelling of the brain, and pneumonia. It is spread by coughing and sneezing (highly contagious). Adapted CDC VARCLL 1000102
Viral haemorragic fevers, not otherwise specified A group of illnesses that are caused by several distinct families of viruses. In general, the term "viral hemorrhagic fever" is used to describe a severe multisystem syndrome. Adapted CDC VRHAEM 1000104
Water-borne illness (2 or more related cases) A range of syndromes, including acute dehydrating diarrhoea (cholera), prolonged febrile illness with abdominal symptoms (typhoid fever), acute bloody diarrhoea (dysentery), and chronic diarrhoea (Brainerd diarrhoea). Usually caused by contaminated surface water sources and large poorly functioning municipal water distribution systems. Adapted CDC WTRBRN 1000105
Yellow fever A tropical virus disease with fever and jaundice, transmitted by the mosquito and often fatal. WHO YELLWF 1000106
Yersinosis (NEC) An infectious disease caused by a bacterium of the genus Yersinia usually acquired by eating contaminated food, especially raw or undercooked pork products. Adapted CDC YERSNS 1000107
Not otherwise specified The appropriate value is not in the set of specified values. ATCCIS NOS 1000108
