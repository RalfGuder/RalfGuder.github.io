Name: military-obstacle-type-category-code
Definition: The specific value that represents the class of MILITARY-OBSTACLE-TYPE.
Definition Source: MIP
Allowable Values:
Value Definition Source Physical Value Identifier
Abatis A vehicular obstacle constructed by felling trees (leaving a 1-2 meter stump above the ground on both sides of a road, trail, gap, or defile) so that they fall, interlocking, toward the expected direction of enemy approach. The trees should remain attached to the stumps, be at a 45 degree angle to the roadway, and the obstacle itself should be at least 75 meters in depth to be most effective. APP-6A OCT 98 ABATIS 1000001
Anti-tank ditch A facility that is a ditch obstacle designed to stop tanks. APP-6A OCT 98 ATDTCH 1000002
Anti-tank obstacle A facility that is an obstacle that is designed or employed to disrupt, fix, turn or block the movement of tanks. APP-6A OCT 98 ANTOBS 1000003
Anti-tank wall A wall-like obstacle capable of stopping tanks. APP-6A OCT 98 - Def MIP-NDAG ANTWAL 1000004
Barbed wire entanglement An obstacle, consisting of twisted wires armed with barbs or sharp points. Adapted USMTF BARBEN 1000005
Barrier, vehicle An obstruction made of vehicles erected to bar the advance of persons or vehicles, or to prevent access to a place. NDAG BARVEH 1000030
Beam post obstacle A squared-off log or a large, oblong piece of timber, metal, or stone inserted in the ground to obstruct movement. Adapted JCS Pub 1-02 BPSOBS 1000006
Crater obstacle A pit or hole in the ground created by an explosion or an impact in order to be used as an obstacle. Adapted OED CRATER 1000007
