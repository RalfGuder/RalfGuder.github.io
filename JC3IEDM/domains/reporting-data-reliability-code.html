Domain
Name: reporting-data-reliability-code
Definition: The specific value that represents, for intelligence purpose, the general appraisal of the source in graded terms to indicate the extent to which it has been proven it can be counted on or trusted in to do as expected.
Definition Source: ATCCIS
Allowable Values:
Value Definition Source Physical Value Identifier
Completely reliable The source of the reported data can be considered as completely reliable, i.e. erroneous information cannot be produced. AIntP-3 A 1000001
Fairly reliable The source of the reported data can be considered as fairly reliable, i.e. erroneous information can be produced, with a probability that cannot be neglected. AIntP-3 C 1000003
Not usually reliable The source of the reported data is not usually reliable, i.e. the probability of producing erroneous information is high (>30%). AIntP-3 D 1000004
Reliability cannot be judged The reliability of the source of the reported data cannot be estimated. AIntP-3 F 1000006
Unreliable The source of the reported data is not reliable, i.e. the produced reported data can generally be considered as erroneous. AIntP-3 E 1000005
Usually reliable The source of the reported data can be considered as usually reliable, i.e. erroneous information can be produced, but the probability of such events can be neglected. AIntP-3 B 1000002
