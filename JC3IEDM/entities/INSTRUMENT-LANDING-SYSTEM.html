Entity
Name: INSTRUMENT-LANDING-SYSTEM
Definition: A MATERIEL that provides aircraft with horizontal and vertical guidance just before landing and during landing, and at certain fixed points, indicates the distance to the reference point of landing.
Supertype: MATERIEL
Attributes:
Name Opt Definition
instrument-landing-system-id (PK) (FK) MA The materiel-id of a specific INSTRUMENT-LANDING-SYSTEM (a role name for object-item-id).
instrument-landing-system-beam-width-dimension OP The one-dimensional linear distance representing the extreme horizontal distance measured from side to side and perpendicular to the central axis of the main beam coil of a specific localiser.
instrument-landing-system-bearing-angle OP The rotational measurement clockwise from true North to the localiser used for final approach guidance.
instrument-landing-system-distance-measuring-equipment-distance-dimension OP The one-dimensional linear distance representing the distance between the distance measuring equipment and the associated landing threshold.
instrument-landing-system-glide-path-angle OP The rotational measurement from the horizontal plane to the glide path, where the positive angle is vertically upward.
instrument-landing-system-localiser-distance-dimension OP The one-dimensional linear distance representing the extreme horizontal distance measured perpendicular from the end of the runway to the localiser antenna position.
instrument-landing-system-magnetic-variation-angle OP The rotational measurement of the angular difference between true north and magnetic north.
instrument-landing-system-slaved-variation-angle OP The rotational measurement of the fixed value of magnetic variation applied to true direction to obtain the magnetic values for radials, courses and headings.
instrument-landing-system-threshold-crossing-height-dimension OP The one-dimensional linear distance representing the height above the landing threshold on a normal glide path.
